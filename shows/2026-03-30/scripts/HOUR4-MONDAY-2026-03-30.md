### SEGMENT 2: THIS DAY IN MUSIC — PJ HARVEY + JOHN DENVER (8:13 AM)
**[WKRP TRANSITION: 3 sec]**

> This day in music. And I have got two stories for you that could not be more different.
>
> First. March thirtieth, nineteen ninety two. PJ Harvey releases her debut album, Dry. Polly Jean Harvey. From Dorset, England. And this record was a grenade. Blues-infused punk rock that was raw and angry and sexual and uncomfortable in a way that made everyone pay attention. She was twenty two. She was playing guitar like she was trying to hurt it. And the music press had no idea what to do with her.
>
> Sheela Na Gig. Dress. Oh My Lover. These songs did not ask permission. They kicked in the door and sat down at the table. Thirty four years later, Dry still sounds like it was recorded in a room that was on fire. If you have never heard it, fix that. Today.
>
> Now. Completely different energy. March thirtieth, nineteen seventy four. John Denver hits number one on the Billboard Hot 100 with Sunshine on My Shoulders. And before you scroll past this because you think John Denver is your grandmother's music, let me make a case.
>
> John Denver was born Henry John Deutschendorf Junior. He changed his name to Denver because he loved Colorado so much he named himself after it. That is commitment. He dropped out of architecture school to play folk music. His father was a test pilot who held speed records. John looked at a man who literally broke the sound barrier and said no thanks, I am going to sing about sunshine.
>
> That is punk rock. Not the sound. The decision. Walking away from everything expected of you to do the thing that makes you feel alive. That is the most punk rock thing I have heard all week.

### SEGMENT 3: THE RANT — STREAMING SERVICES (8:28 AM)
**[WKRP TRANSITION: 3 sec]**

> The rant. The last rant. I have been saving the best for last.
>
> Streaming services. We need to talk about streaming services. Because at some point in the last ten years, cutting the cord went from saving money to spending exactly the same amount of money on twelve different apps instead of one cable bill.
>
> Netflix. Hulu. Disney Plus. HBO Max. Or is it just Max now. Or was it Max and now it is something else. I cannot keep track. Amazon Prime which somehow includes free shipping and a Lord of the Rings show that cost more than the GDP of several countries. Peacock. Paramount Plus. Apple TV Plus. Tubi. Pluto. I think there is one called BritBox which sounds like a shipping container.
>
> And every single one of them has that one show. That one show you need that app for. So you subscribe. You watch the show. And then you forget to cancel. For eight months. And you are paying twelve dollars a month for a service you used once. Congratulations. You are a patron of the arts. Against your will.
>
> Meanwhile, every show you actually want to watch is on a different service than the one you are currently looking at. You spend forty five minutes searching for something to watch across seven apps and then you put on The Office again. Which is on Peacock now. Which you forgot you were paying for.
>
> I am going back to radio. Wait. I am already here. Problem solved.
