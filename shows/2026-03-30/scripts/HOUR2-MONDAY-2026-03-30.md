### SEGMENT 2: THIS DAY IN MUSIC — CLAPTON BIRTHDAY SPECIAL (6:13 AM)
**[WKRP TRANSITION: 3 sec]**

> This day in music. And today is a big one. March thirtieth, nineteen forty five. Eric Patrick Clapton is born in Ripley, Surrey, England. He is eighty one years old today. Eighty one.
>
> Now let me tell you what makes Clapton's story actually interesting. He was raised by his grandparents thinking they were his parents. His mother was sixteen when she had him. His father was a Canadian soldier who had already gone home. He did not learn the truth until he was nine years old. Nine. That is the kind of origin story that either makes you a supervillain or a blues guitarist. He went with option two.
>
> He got his first guitar at thirteen. By twenty he was in the Yardbirds. By twenty one he had quit the Yardbirds because they were too commercial. At twenty one. Most of us at twenty one were trying to figure out how to do laundry. Clapton was already too cool for a hit band.
>
> Then Cream. Then Blind Faith. Then Derek and the Dominos. Then solo. The man has reinvented himself more times than Madonna. And through all of it, through the drugs, through the tragedy, through the ego, through the genuinely terrible periods where he made some choices we will not get into, the guitar never lied. When Clapton plays, you hear every single thing he has ever been through.
>
> Happy birthday, Slowhand. Here is some more of your work.

### SEGMENT 3: THE RANT — SHOPPING CART THEORY (6:28 AM)
**[WKRP TRANSITION: 3 sec]**

> The rant. Hour two edition. And I promised you this one.
>
> The Shopping Cart Theory. If you have not heard of this, it is very simple. There is no law that says you have to return your shopping cart to the corral. Nobody is going to arrest you. Nobody is going to fine you. There is no consequence whatsoever for leaving your cart in the middle of the parking lot like an abandoned spaceship.
>
> Which means returning the cart is the ultimate test of whether a person is a good person. It is objectively the right thing to do. It is easy to do. It benefits everyone. And nobody is watching. There is no reward. There is no punishment. It is just you, the cart, and your soul.
>
> And some of you leave that cart right next to your car. Just roll it two feet to the left and walk away. I have seen you. We have all seen you. The cart corral is twenty steps away. Twenty steps. That is the distance between being a functioning member of society and being a person who parks a metal rectangle in a handicap spot because the walk was too far.
>
> The shopping cart is the litmus test of civilization. And I am sorry to report that based on every parking lot I have ever visited, civilization is losing.
>
> You know who returns their cart? People who listen to this station. That is not a fact. But I believe it.
