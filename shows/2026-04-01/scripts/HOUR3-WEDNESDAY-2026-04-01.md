### SEGMENT: THE RANT

Alright. Can we talk about April Fools Day for a second. Because I need to get this off my chest. When did April Fools Day become a competitive sport. It used to be you put a rubber spider on somebody's desk. Maybe you saran-wrapped the toilet. Simple. Classic. Elegant. Now people are out here faking engagements on social media. Companies are announcing fake products. Your cousin posts that she is moving to Portugal and you have to spend forty-five minutes figuring out if it is real. I saw a guy last year announce he quit his job on April first. His boss believed him. Cleaned out his desk. He showed up Thursday and his stuff was in the parking lot. That is not a prank. That is unemployment. And the corporate ones. Oh the corporate ones. Every company thinks they are hilarious today. They are not. Nobody wants bacon-flavored toothpaste. Nobody wants a dating app for your pets. Just sell me the thing I already buy and leave me alone. The only acceptable April Fools prank is a good one. And a good one requires two things. It has to be believable for exactly five seconds. And it has to be harmless. That is it. That is the whole formula. If your prank requires an apology afterward it was not a prank. It was just being a jerk with a calendar excuse. Anyway. Be careful out there today. Trust nothing. Here is some music you can trust.
