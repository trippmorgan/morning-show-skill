### SEGMENT: GRUNGE WEDNESDAY PREVIEW

Alright. Tonight. Grunge Wednesday with Tripp. Five PM to eight PM. WPFQ ninety-six point seven. Let me tell you why you should care even if you think you do not care about grunge. First of all, grunge is not a museum piece. It is not nostalgia night at a theme restaurant. Those records still sound raw and alive and angry in a way that most modern rock has forgotten how to be. And Tripp gets that. He does not just throw on Ten and Nevermind and coast. He will play you Tad. He will play you the Melvins. He will drop a Screaming Trees deep cut and then follow it with Alice in Chains Unplugged and you will feel things you did not plan on feeling on a Wednesday evening. The man curates. He builds a three-hour arc from the muddy basements of Seattle to the arenas and back down again. And you know what, April first nineteen ninety-four was right around when Kurt Cobain was in his final days. So tonight carries some weight if you know the history. Tripp knows the history. Five PM. ### SEGMENT: THE RANT

Can we talk about the word hustle for a minute. I am so tired of the word hustle. Everything is a hustle now. Side hustle. Morning hustle routine. Hustle culture. Grind and hustle. When I was growing up hustle meant one of two things. Either you were running a con or you were doing a disco dance. Both were at least interesting. Now it just means working yourself into the ground and posting about it online like that is a personality. I saw a guy on the internet the other day who said he wakes up at three thirty AM to optimize his day. Three thirty. My alarm goes off at four fifteen and I consider that a violation of the Geneva Convention. This man is up an hour before me by choice doing cold plunges and journaling and drinking something green. You know what I drink at four fifteen. Coffee. Black. From a machine that I am not entirely sure I have cleaned this decade. And I am not hustling. I am surviving. There is a difference. If your morning routine takes longer than an hour you do not have a routine. You have a part-time job you are not getting paid for. Alright. Music. Before I spiral further.
