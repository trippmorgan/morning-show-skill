### SEGMENT: THE WILDCARD

Alright. It is Wednesday. And on Wednesdays I reserve the right to play something that does not technically belong on rock radio and explain to you why it absolutely does. Today's wildcard. I am going with Stevie Ray Vaughan. Now some of you are saying Fever, that is just blues. And you are right. It is blues. But here is the thing. Every guitar player you love — every single one — stole something from the blues. Hendrix knew it. Clapton knew it. Page knew it. The guys from Seattle who invented grunge knew it. If you trace the DNA of any electric guitar tone you care about, it runs through the Mississippi Delta and through the fingers of people like Stevie Ray. The man played guitar like it owed him money. Technical perfection married to raw emotion and that combination does not come around often. He died at thirty-five in a helicopter crash in nineteen ninety. Thirty-five years old. Think about what he accomplished in thirty-five years and then think about what I have done this morning which is drink coffee and argue with a password screen. Perspective. Here is Stevie Ray Vaughan and if this does not move you I cannot help you.

### SEGMENT: THE RANT

Wednesday. Hump day. The summit. Since we are at the peak of the week I am going to talk about something that has been building in me. Group chats. I need to address group chats. I am currently in — and I counted this morning — eleven group chats. Eleven. I did not ask to join any of them. I was added. Silently. Without my knowledge or consent. And now my phone buzzes three hundred times a day with conversations I did not start about topics I do not care about between people who apparently have unlimited time and unlimited opinions about where to eat lunch. There is one group chat that has not had a relevant message in four months but nobody will leave because apparently leaving a group chat is the modern equivalent of walking out of a dinner party. It is a social contract I never signed. And the worst part. The absolute worst part. Someone sends a message at eleven PM and it starts a chain reaction that goes until one in the morning. My phone is vibrating on the nightstand like it is trying to escape the room. I am lying there in the dark watching notifications scroll past like a stock ticker for information I will never need. I am leaving all eleven group chats. Today. April first. And when people ask if it is an April Fools' joke I will say yes. But I will not rejoin. That is the long game. Here is music before I burst something.
