### SEGMENT: MUSIC HISTORY

On this day. April first. In nineteen eighty-four, Marvin Gaye was shot and killed by his own father. One day before his forty-fifth birthday. I do not care how many times I say that sentence. It never stops being one of the most tragic things in music history. The man gave us What's Going On. Let's Get It On. I Heard It Through the Grapevine. Songs that are woven into the actual fabric of what music is. If you have never listened to What's Going On as a full album, front to back, today is the day. It holds up. It more than holds up. Also on this day in nineteen eighty-five, David Lee Roth officially walked out of Van Halen. Six albums deep and gone. And the argument over whether Van Halen was better with Dave or Sammy has raged ever since. I have an opinion. I will keep it to myself. No I will not. It is Dave. And in nineteen eighty-nine, The Bangles hit number one with Eternal Flame. Walk like an Egyptian gets the attention but Eternal Flame is the better song and I will die on that hill. Speaking of things worth fighting for.

### SEGMENT: THE RANT

Can we talk about April Fools' Day for a minute. I know some of you woke up this morning already on high alert. Checking every text message. Squinting at every headline. And I get it. Because the internet has turned April first into an absolute minefield. Every company announces a fake product. Every social media account posts something unbelievable. And the problem is that actual news has gotten so bizarre over the last few years that you cannot tell the difference anymore. A headline that says man robs six banks in five days and makes six hundred dollars. Is that a prank or is that just a very bad business plan that actually happened. I will tell you — that actually happened. Six banks. Six hundred bucks. That is a hundred dollars a bank. The self-checkout machine at the grocery store has better returns than that. We have lost the ability to separate satire from reality and April first is just the one day we all admit it. So here is my promise. Nothing I say today is a prank. The music is real. The weather is real. My displeasure at being awake is profoundly real. The only joke here is that I do this voluntarily. Here is music.
