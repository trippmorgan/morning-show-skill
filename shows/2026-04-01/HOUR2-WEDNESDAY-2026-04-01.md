### SEGMENT: GRUNGE WEDNESDAY PREVIEW

Alright. Let me talk about tonight for a minute. Grunge Wednesday. Tripp. Five PM to eight PM on this very station. If you have not tuned in for one of these yet you are missing something and I do not say that about many things. Here is what Tripp does that sets it apart. He does not just play the hits. Anybody can queue up Smells Like Teen Spirit and call it a night. Tripp digs. He plays the B-sides. The deep album cuts. The bands that never quite broke through but absolutely should have. He will play Mudhoney next to Nirvana. He will drop a Screaming Trees track and then explain why Mark Lanegan's voice was a once-in-a-generation thing. And yeah he plays the big ones too because those songs are big for a reason. Grunge was not just a sound. It was a whole generation of people saying we are done pretending everything is fine. And that still resonates. It resonated in nineteen ninety-one and it resonates right here in twenty-six sitting in Albany Georgia on an April morning. Tonight. Five PM. Tripp. WPFQ ninety-six point seven. Wear flannel if you have got it. Here is something to get you in the mood.

### SEGMENT: THE RANT

You know what I have been thinking about. Passwords. I tried to log into something this morning. Does not matter what. And it told me my password was too weak. So I made it stronger. Then it needed a special character. So I added one. Then it said I had used this password before. No I had not. I used a similar password in two thousand nineteen for a completely different website. And somehow the internet remembers that but cannot remember that I already own the shoes it keeps showing me ads for. I am wearing the shoes. I have the shoes. Stop showing me the shoes. And now every site wants two-factor authentication which means I need my phone to unlock my computer to check my email to reset the password I forgot because it had to be sixteen characters with a hieroglyphic and the maiden name of my first pet's veterinarian. I have a notebook. An actual paper notebook. With a pen. All my passwords are in there in my own handwriting. And if someone breaks into my house and finds it, honestly, they have earned it at that point. That is a heist movie I would watch. Here is music. My blood pressure needs it.

### SEGMENT: QUICK HITS

Quick hits hour two. Birthdays we have not covered yet. Jeff Porcaro of Toto was born on this day in nineteen fifty-four. Would have been seventy-two today. Now I know you hear Toto and think Africa and that is fine but Jeff Porcaro was one of the greatest studio drummers who ever lived. The man played on everything. Steely Dan. Boz Scaggs. Michael Jackson's Thriller. Listen to Rosanna — which we just played and that was not an accident — and tell me that groove is not perfection. You cannot. Because it is. David Oyelowo turns fifty today. Played Martin Luther King Junior in Selma and was extraordinary. If you have not seen that film, this weekend is your assignment. In sports, the Braves have opened the season and we are in that beautiful early phase where anything is possible and the math has not crushed our spirits yet. Spencer Strider is on the injured list with an oblique strain which is not great but Chris Sale and Reynaldo Lopez are dealing so far. Final Four this weekend — UConn versus Illinois and Arizona versus Michigan. And here in Albany the azaleas are starting to show out. Drive through some of the older neighborhoods if you want free entertainment. They are putting on a performance.
